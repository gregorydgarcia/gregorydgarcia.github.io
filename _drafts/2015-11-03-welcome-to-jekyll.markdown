# Making TogBro Part 1: Planning
## Background
Growing up as an only child I had the opportunity that everything I had was purchased specifically for me. My clothes fit perfect because there was no big brother to hand down his 4 year out of style clothes. There was no notebooks with their pages half torn out. Or gold clubs regripped for a specific swing. 

This is probably why I can't stand tools that are made to appeal to a larger audience. I understand that a company can't fundamentally succeed if they have to support a new tool for everyone. The joy is with number of tutorials, libraries, and APIs building you own software specifically for you is easier than ever. 

This is the planning article on what I consider to be a better time tracking alternative made specifically for my roll in my company. I'm not trying to consider different end users. I am considering myself and about 30 other people who may choose to use the tool. It takes a certain level of nurocese to feel the need to rebuild what is already a great tool just for yourself but I can't help but customize it to my need.
